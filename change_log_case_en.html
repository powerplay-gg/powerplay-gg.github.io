Loginet, 2021

Entity logging tool

Problem

Users cannot independently find out why an entity is in its current state and how it got there. They write and call to support, thereby heavily loading it.

Task

Create a logging tool so that users can independently find out how and why the entity is in the current status.

Solution

First, it was necessary to decide what information users want to see. We interviewed them and found out that we need to reflect the action, who performed it, the date and time, and the status change.

There was already something similar on the product, but only in the shipmend card. This solution does not scale to the entire product, it eats up part of the usable space and is not very informative. I decided not to expand this idea and continued to think what could be done.

Current implementation

All entities on the portal in view mode have a header with tabs that duplicate the main blocks of the entity. Tabs are a great way to put the changelog in there. All entities have them, under you we have a lot of space to display the necessary information and we do not take up useful space from the card.

The only thing left is to design it. Having made several options, I showed them to several colleagues and friends. As I expected, 3rd and 4th options were dismissed, because. graphically does not look very attractive, option 2 seemed more complex and incomprehensible. Stopped at the first one.

Final option

Result

After the release, the load on support decreased, calls like “Why don’t I see the shipment in the auction, I distributed it there” reduced to almost zero
