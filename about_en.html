Backstory

In 2019, I have been working in sales for five years. I realized that I was standing in one place and I needed to change a profession. I chose the IT, because it is a endless movement, development and technology. Design turned out to be the closest direction to me. At first I studied everything on my own, then I went to the WDI school courses, got into a product company and began my professional path in design.

Loginet

In almost two years of work at Loginet, I redesigned more than 10 services, designed 5 new services, made a new admin panel, rebuilt and structured a style guide, rewrote half of the design guidlines. After my manager left, I had to take the managment into my own hands, hired employees, built key processes within the department (sprints, 1:1, design debt, guidelines), began to join product development discussions with CTO, PO and founders. We also managed to convince the founders of the need for CJM (we made it together with the team and began to close the pains that it highlighted). It turned out to shift the company to a data-driven approach in design and start changing the processes of product creation. For example, now developers are connecting an analytics system for the portal; when developing complex services, the roadmap includes time for testing design before being transferred to development.
